6.1 Lemma 1

Lemma 1: For an arbitrary foreign (home) trade policy, \(\tau_j\), let \(\tau_i^0\) denote the government’s optimal policy in the absence of political contributions (\(C_i(\tau_i, \tau_j) = 0\)) and \(g_i(\tau_i; \tau_j)\) its associated welfare. \[ \tau_i^0 \in \underset{\tau_i}{\operatorname{argmax}} g_i(\tau_i; \tau_j) \] In a domestic political economic equilibrium, the government chooses trade policy to maximize a weighted sum of producer and social welfare \[ \tau_i^\star \in \underset{\tau_i}{\operatorname{argmax}} \Pi_i(\tau_i, \tau_j) + a S_i(\tau_i, \tau_j) + r(\tau_i, \tau_j) \] and equilibrium political contributions from producers of Good 1 (\(C_i^\star\)) satisfy \[ C_i^\star(\tau_i^0, \tau_i^\star; \tau_j) = \Pi_i(\tau_i^\star, \tau_j) + a S_i(\tau_i^\star, \tau_j) + r(\tau_i^\star, \tau_j) - g_i(\tau_i^0; \tau_j) \]

Proof: Suppose \(\tau_i^\star\) does not maximize joint welfare. Then lobby can revise its contribution schedule to generate greater joint welfare and give the government \(\epsilon\) of the surplus. It follows that \(\tau_i^\star\) must solve insert reference. Taking this policy as given, equilibrium contributions will make government indifferent between implementing \(\tau_i^\star\) and \(\tau_i^0\). If \(C_i < C_i^\star\), then government doesn’t prefer to implement optimal policies. If \(C_i > C_i^\star\) then lobby can contribute \(\epsilon\) less and increase its net welfare.

6.2 Lemma 2

Lemma 2: In any take-it-or-leave-it bargaining game in which trade wars \(\left\{\tau_i^\star, \tau_j^\star \right\}\) (Def. 2) serves as the reversion point, governments will implement \(\left\{\tau_i^t, \tau_j^t \right\}\) where \[ \left\{\tau_i^t, \tau_j^t \right\} \in \underset{\left\{\tau_i, \tau_j \right\}}{\operatorname{argmax}} G_i(\tau_i, \tau_j; a_i, a_j) + G_j(\tau_i, \tau_j; a_i, a_j) \] Government \(i\)’s side payment to \(j\) satisfies \[ R_j(a_j) = G_j(\tau_i^t, \tau_j^t; a_j) - G_j(\tau_i^\star, \tau_j^\star; a_j) \] yielding utility \[ \overline{G}_i(\tau_i^t, \tau_j^t; a_i, a_j) = G_i(\tau_i^t, \tau_j^t; a_i) - R_j \]

Proof: (actually follows Lemma 1 closely). First part follows from joint welfare maximization. If offer didn’t maximize joint welfare I could rearrange and give myself \(\epsilon\) more. Side payment makes the receiver of the offer indifferent between accepting and rejecting, given the ‘hot wars’ outside option. Anything more and I can keep \(\epsilon\) for myself and \(j\) will still accept. Anything less and \(j\) goes to war, clearly inefficient.

6.3 Lemma 3

Lemma 3:

3.1 \(\tau_i^\star(a_i) = \underset{\tau_j}{\operatorname{argmax}} G_i(\tau_i; a_i)\) is strictly decreasing in \(a_i\).

Proof: \[ G(\tau_i; a_i) = \Pi(\tau_i) + a_i S(\tau_i) + r(\tau_i) \] with \(a_i \in [0,1]\). Note first that \(S(\tau_i)\) is a decreasing function of \(\tau_i\). Fix \(\tau_i\) and consider an arbitrary \(\left\{a_i, a_i^\prime \right\}\) with \(a_i^\prime > a_i\). It is immediate that \(G(\tau_i; a_i^\prime) < G(\tau_i; a_i)\) pointwise \(\implies \tau_i^\star(a_i^prime) < \tau_i(a_i)\). Set of maximizing \(\tau_i^\star\) is ordered and decreasing in \(a_i\).

3.2 There is a one-to-one mapping between changes in a foreign government’s type (\(a_j\)) and changes in home (\(p_i\)) and international (\(\pi\)) prices in a Trade Wars equilibrium (Def. 2).

Proof: The ratio \(\tau_i / \tau_j\) is sufficient to determine \(\pi\) (GH95). \(i\)’s best response function is an increasing an concave function of \(\tau_j\) (need to show this). By Lemma 3.1, increases in \(a_j\) cause decreases in \(\tau_j^\star\). Consider \(a_j < a_j^\prime\) and induced \(\tau_j^\star\) in trade wars. By increasingness of home BR, \(\tau_i^\star(a_j) > \tau_i(a_j^\prime)\), but by concaveness of BR \(\pi(a_j) < \pi(a_j^\prime)\) (ratio of tariffs increases (decreases?)). This implies \(p_i^\star(a_j) < p_i^\star(a_j^\prime)\) (domestic prices fall in response to increase in foreign tariff - equiproportionate increase in home tariff (linear BR) would keep home prices the same). So in trade wars, $ a_j { p_i, }$ which is sufficient to infer home welfare changes to changes in foreign government’s type.

Proof: \(F\) induces distribution of \(\rho \in [0,1]\). Change in tariffs from moving from trade wars to trade talks is constant for all \(a_i, a_j \in [0,1]\) by Grossman Helpman 1995 Eq. 16. So \(\Delta G\) from moving from trade wars to trade talks is a constant percentage of \(\Gamma\) for all \(a_i\). Define \(\omega_j^{\star i}\) to be solve eq. insert reference with equality \[ \left[ \rho_i(\omega_i, \omega_j^\star) G_i^w(a_i, a_j^{\star i}) + (1 - \rho_i(\omega_i, \omega_j^\star) G_i^w(a_i^{\star j}, a_j) \right] ( 1 - c_i ) = G_i^w(a_i, a_j) \] This gives point at which \(j\)’s capabilities make \(i\) indifferent between trade wars and hot wars.
