2.1 Economic Equilibrium

Countries are labeled 1 and 2 and produce two goods, labeled 0 and 1. For exposition here, I focus on the ‘home’ country, indexed with \(i\). Primitives in the ‘foreign’ country are defined analagously. Each country hosts a representative consumer, which holds quasilinear utility \(U_i\) over the consumption of two goods, where \(q_{i0}\) denotes country \(i\)’s consumer’s consumption of good 0 and \(u_i\) is some increasing and concave function with \(u_i(0) = 0\). Consumer utility:
Ui(qi)=qi0 + ui(qi1)
 The consumer is endowed with \(L_i\) units of labor which it can devote to the production of either good 0 or good 1, earning wages \(w_{i0}\) and \(w_{i1}\) respectively.

Good 0 serves as the numeraire (\(p_{i0} = 1\)) and is produced using labor only with constant returns to scale. To normalize the competitve wage to zero, I let \(f_{i0}^\prime(L_{i0}) = 1\). Production of numeraire good:
fi0(Li0)
Normalization of the competitive wage gives wi0 = wi1 = 1 and simplifies the consumer’s budget constraint to
q0 + piqi1 ≤ Li
 Good 1 is produced with both labor and some specific factor. The producers of good 1 own \(K_{i1}\) units of this specific factor, and produce \(q_{i1}\) units of the good using technology \(f_{i1}(L_{i1}, K_{i1})\). Production of Good 1:
fi1(Li1, Ki1)

Domestic prices for good 1, \(p_{i}\), are the product of the international price \(\pi\) and the domestic trade policy \(\tau_{i}\):6 Note that I suppress the good index, as prices and trade in good 1 will serve as the object of analysis for the remainder of the paper. \[ p_i = \tau_i \pi \]

Note that this formulation allows for domestic prices to be affected by both tariffs and subsidies. For an importer of good 1, \(\tau_i = 1\) represents free trade, \(\tau_i > 1\) a tariff, and \(\tau_i < 1\) a subsidy.7 The opposite holds for an exporter.

In a competitive equilibrium, consumers maximize their utility subject to their budget constraints, producers maximize profits subject to production feasibility, domestic labor markets clear, and international good markets clear.8 This holds for an arbitrary bilateral trade policy \(\left\{ \tau_i, \tau_j \right\}\) This yields Definition 1. Producers of the numeraire good maximize fi0(Li0)−Li0. Since fi0(Li0) exhibits constant returns to scale, we have that fi0(Li0)=fi0′(Li0)Li0 and since fi0′(Li0)=1, numeraire producers make zero profits in equilibrium and it is sufficient to consider the profit maximization problem of producers of Good 1.

Definition 1: In a competitive equilibrium, consumers in both countries maximize their utility, subject to their budget constraint. \[ \begin{align*} \max_{\boldsymbol{q}} && U_i(\boldsymbol{q}) \\ \text{subject to } && q_0 + p_i q_{i1} \leq L_i \end{align*} \] Producers maximize their profits. \[ \max_{\boldsymbol{L_{i1}}} \quad p_i f_{i1}(L_{i1}, K_{i1}) - L_{i1} \] Domestic labor markets clear. \[ L_{i0} + L_{i1} = L_i \] And international good markets clear. \[ q_{10} + q_{20} = f_{10}(L_{10}) + f_{20}(L_{20}) \\ \] \[ q_{11} + q_{21} = f_{11}(L_{11}, K_{11}) + f_{21}(L_{21}, K_{21}) \]

These conditions jointly determine the international price, \(\pi\), and trade between the countries. I denote the consumer demand for Good 1 induced by maximization of \(U_i\) with \(d_{i}(p_i)\), the supply curve induced by profit maximization with \(X_i(p_i)\), and net imports to country \(i\) with \(m_i(p_i)\). Net Imports:
mi(pi)=di(pi)−Xi(pi)
 Tariff revenue, \(r(\tau_i, \pi)\), is simply the product of the ad valorem tariff and net imports. Tariff Revenue:
r(τi, π)=(τi − 1)⋅π ⋅ mi(τi * π)

How do producers and consumers fare under a given equilibrium? For welfare analysis, I consider the surplus that accrues to consumers and the profits that accrue to producers of the non-numeraire good. Consumer surplus is given by \[ S(p_i) = U_i(\boldsymbol{q}_i^\star) - p_i q_{i1}^\star - q_{i0}^\star \] where \(\boldsymbol{q}_i^\star\) is equilibrium consumption. Producer profits are given by \[ \Pi(p_i) = p_i \cdot f_{i1}(L_{i1}^\star, K_{i1}) - L_{i1}^\star(p_i) \] Note the conflict of interest between domestic producers and consumers. Consumers naturally prefer a lower price for the non-numeraire good, while producers prefer a higher price.9 Need to show price effect dominates labor market effect.
